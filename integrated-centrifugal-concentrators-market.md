<p><ul><li>Concentrator With Rotor</li><li>Concentrator Without Rotor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Integrated Centrifugal Concentrators market is categorized into two main types: concentrators with rotors and those without rotors. Concentrators with rotors utilize a spinning mechanism to enhance separation efficiency, effectively separating particles based on density. In contrast, concentrators without rotors rely on gravitational forces and static processes for particle concentration. Both types cater to different industrial applications, with rotor-based models typically offering higher throughput and efficiency, while non-rotor models may provide cost advantages in specific scenarios.</p></p>

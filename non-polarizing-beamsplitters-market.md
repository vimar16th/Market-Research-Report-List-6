<p><p>Non-polarizing beamsplitters are optical devices used to divide incident light into two or more separate beams without changing the polarization state. There are two main types - non-polarizing plate beamsplitters and non-polarizing cube beamsplitters. Plate beamsplitters use a thin coating on a glass substrate to split light, while cube beamsplitters use a combination of multiple reflecting surfaces within a cube. Both types are widely used in various applications such as microscopy, interferometry, and spectroscopy due to their ability to split light evenly without altering its polarization.</p></p>

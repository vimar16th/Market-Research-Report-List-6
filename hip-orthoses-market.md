<p><p>Hip orthoses are devices used to support and stabilize the hip joint in various conditions such as fractures, dislocations, or arthritis. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide continuous motion and support to the hip joint, promoting healing and mobility. On the other hand, static orthotics are meant to immobilize the joint, limiting movement and providing stability. Both types of orthoses serve different purposes in aiding in hip joint function and healing, and are chosen based on the specific needs of the patient.</p></p>

<p><p>The pharmaceutical starch market comprises various types, including corn starch, potato starch, and others. Corn starch, derived from maize, is widely used for its excellent binding and disintegrating properties in tablet formulations. Potato starch is known for its superior viscosity and thickening capabilities, making it ideal for suspensions and emulsions. Other starch types may include tapioca and wheat starch, each offering unique functional benefits. The selection of starch type depends on the desired properties and application in pharmaceutical formulations.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Airlaid</li><li>Wetlaid</li><li>Spunlaid</li><li>Meltblown</li><li>Needlepunching</li><li>Hydro-entanglement</li><li>Thermobonded</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nonwovens for filtration market includes various types: </p><p>1. **Airlaid**: Uses air to form a web, known for its absorbency and softness.</p><p>2. **Wetlaid**: Involves water to create strong, uniform sheets, ideal for liquid filtration.</p><p>3. **Spunlaid**: Employs spun fibers for strong, lightweight fabrics used in air filtration.</p><p>4. **Meltblown**: Utilizes melted polymer to produce fine fibers, excellent for particle filtration.</p><p>5. **Needlepunching**: Interlocks fibers mechanically for durability. </p><p>6. **Hydro-entanglement**: Uses high-pressure water jets to bond fibers.</p><p>7. **Thermobonded**: Employs heat to bond fibers, enhancing strength and stability for filtration applications.</p></p>

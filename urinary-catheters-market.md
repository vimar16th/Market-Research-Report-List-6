<p><p>The urinary catheters market is primarily divided into three types: Foley catheters, intermittent catheters, and male external catheters. Foley catheters are indwelling devices that remain in the bladder for prolonged use, often in hospital settings. Intermittent catheters are used on a temporary basis for self-catheterization, favored for home care and short-term needs. Male external catheters, also known as condom catheters, are non-invasive options designed to fit externally, providing comfort and ease of use for men with urinary incontinence.</p></p>

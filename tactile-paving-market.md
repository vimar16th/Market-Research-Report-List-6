<p><p>Tactile paving is divided into different types based on the markings present on the surface to aid visually impaired individuals. Ribs marking consists of long, parallel lines that indicate a safe path to follow. Raised dots marking consists of raised, circular bumps that signal potential hazards or changes in direction. Other types of tactile paving may include different patterns, textures, or symbols to provide additional information or warnings to pedestrians. These various markings serve as important navigational tools for people with visual impairments.</p></p>

<p><p>Current collectors for rail transit are devices used to collect electricity from overhead wires or third rails to power trains. There are three main types of current collectors in the market: top-running, bottom-running, and side-running. Top-running collectors are mounted on top of the train and collect electricity from overhead wires. Bottom-running collectors are mounted underneath the train and collect electricity from a third rail. Side-running collectors are mounted on the side of the train and collect electricity from parallel wires or rails. Each type has its own advantages and is suitable for different types of rail transit systems.</p></p>

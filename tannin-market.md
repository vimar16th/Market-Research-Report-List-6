<p><ul><li>Hydrolysable Tannin</li><li>Non-Hydrolysable Tannin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The tannin market is primarily divided into hydrolysable and non-hydrolysable tannins. Hydrolysable tannins are soluble in water and can break down into smaller compounds through hydrolysis, often sourced from plants like oak and gall. They are commonly used in the food, wine, and pharmaceutical industries. Non-hydrolysable tannins, on the other hand, are more stable and resistant to hydrolysis, derived primarily from sources such as condensed tannins in certain legumes. They find applications in leather production and various industrial uses.</p></p>

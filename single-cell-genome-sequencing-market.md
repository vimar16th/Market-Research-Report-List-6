<p><p>The single-cell genome sequencing market consists of various technologies for analyzing individual cells' genetic material. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) enables high-throughput sequencing for comprehensive genomic insights. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies specific DNA segments for targeted analysis, while qPCR (quantitative PCR) quantifies DNA, allowing for measuring expression levels. Microarrays facilitate simultaneous analysis of thousands of genes, and MDA (Multiple Displacement Amplification) is a technique for amplifying genomic material from single cells, enhancing the detection of rare variants. Each method offers unique advantages for single-cell studies.</p></p>

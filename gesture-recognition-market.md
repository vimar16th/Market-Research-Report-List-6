<p><p>The gesture recognition market is primarily divided into two types: 2D and 3D gesture recognition. </p><p>2D gesture recognition focuses on interpreting movements on a flat surface, typically using cameras or touchscreens, enabling applications in smartphones, tablets, and interactive kiosks. </p><p>In contrast, 3D gesture recognition perceives spatial movements in a three-dimensional environment, relying on devices like depth sensors or motion capture systems, allowing for more immersive interactions in gaming, virtual reality, and smart home controls. Both technologies enhance user experiences across various industries.</p></p>

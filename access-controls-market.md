<p><p>Access control markets can be categorized primarily into card-based systems and biometric systems. Card-based access controls utilize physical cards equipped with magnetic strips, RFID, or smart technology to grant entry, offering a convenient yet manageable way to secure entrances. In contrast, biometric systems leverage unique physiological traits, such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris patterns, enhancing security through individual identification. Both market types address the need for effective authentication and control in various settings, including commercial and residential environments.</p></p>

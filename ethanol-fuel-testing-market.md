<p><p>Ethanol fuel testing involves various market types to ensure the quality of ethanol fuel. This includes testing for ethanol purity, methanol content, solvent washed gums, water content, denaturant content, and inorganic chloride content. Ethanol purity testing ensures the ethanol meets specified standards, while methanol content testing checks for the presence of methanol, a potential contaminant. Solvent washed gums testing assesses the amount of insoluble substances in the fuel, while water content testing ensures there is no excess water present. Denaturant content testing evaluates the level of denaturants added to the ethanol, and inorganic chloride content testing checks for the presence of chloride ions which can corrode fuel system components.</p></p>

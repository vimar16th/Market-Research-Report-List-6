<p><p>Bedside monitors are categorized into three main types based on the level of acuity they cater to. High-acuity monitors are designed for critical care units and provide advanced monitoring capabilities for patients in critical condition. Mid-acuity monitors are commonly used in step-down units or general wards, offering a moderate level of monitoring for patients who require closer observation. Low-acuity monitors are typically found in general wards or outpatient settings, providing basic monitoring functions for stable patients. Each type of monitor is tailored to meet the specific needs of patients across different acuity levels.</p></p>

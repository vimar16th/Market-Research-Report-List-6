<p><p>InGaAs cameras are categorized into cooled and uncooled types based on their thermal management. Cooled InGaAs cameras employ cooling mechanisms to reduce thermal noise, enhancing sensitivity and enabling high-performance imaging in low-light or infrared applications. These are typically used in scientific research, spectroscopy, and industrial inspections. Conversely, uncooled InGaAs cameras operate at ambient temperatures, making them more compact and cost-effective for applications such as machine vision and monitoring. Both types cater to diverse market needs in various sectors.</p></p>

<p><p>Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) come in two main types: semi-automated and fully automated. Semi-automated AEDs require the user to push a button to deliver a shock to the patient, while fully automated AEDs deliver the shock automatically without any input from the user. Both types of AEDs are designed to quickly and effectively deliver a life-saving shock to a person experiencing sudden cardiac arrest. The choice between semi-automated and fully automated AEDs often depends on the user's comfort level and training.</p></p>

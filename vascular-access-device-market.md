<p><ul><li>Central Vascular Access Devices</li><li>Peripheral Vascular Access Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The vascular access device market is categorized into central and peripheral vascular access devices. Central vascular access devices (CVADs) are designed for long-term use, providing access to large blood vessels for administration of medications, nutrients, or fluids. They are often used in patients requiring intensive or prolonged treatment. Peripheral vascular access devices (PVADs), on the other hand, are typically used for short-term access to smaller veins, suitable for administering medications or fluids briefly, like during outpatient procedures.</p></p>

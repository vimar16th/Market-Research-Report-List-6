<p><p>The cooled infrared detector market comprises several types of detectors, each serving unique applications. Photoconductive detectors, like HgCdTe, exhibit changes in resistance when exposed to infrared radiation, making them sensitive in various environments. Photovoltaic detectors generate voltage in response to infrared light, offering high efficiency. Schottky barrier detectors utilize metal-semiconductor junctions for fast response times. Quantum well detectors leverage thin-film structures for enhanced performance. Other detectors may include innovative designs and materials that cater to specific needs in infrared sensing applications.</p></p>
